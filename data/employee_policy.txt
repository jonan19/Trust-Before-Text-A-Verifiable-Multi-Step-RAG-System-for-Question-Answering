ACME Corporation — Employee Policy Handbook
===========================================
Version 3.1 | Effective January 2024

Section 1: Leave Policy
-----------------------
Employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
Leave entitlement accrues monthly at a rate of 1.67 days per month.
Unused leave of up to 10 days may be carried forward to the next calendar year.
All leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 5 working days in advance.
Emergency leave of up to 3 days may be taken without prior approval but must be reported to the line manager on the first day of absence.

Section 2: Remote Work Policy
------------------------------
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week subject to manager approval.
Remote work eligibility applies to employees who have completed at least 6 months of service.
A formal remote work agreement must be signed and submitted to HR before commencing any remote arrangement.
Employees working remotely are expected to be available during core hours of 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM in their local time zone.
All remote workers must ensure a secure internet connection and adhere to the company's data security policy.

Section 3: Compensation and Salary Reviews
-------------------------------------------
Salary reviews are conducted twice a year, in April and October.
Performance ratings from the preceding 6-month appraisal cycle are the primary input for salary adjustment decisions.
Merit increases are awarded based on a four-tier rating: Exceptional, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory.
Employees rated Exceptional may receive a merit increase of up to 15% of their base salary.
Employees rated Meets Expectations may receive a merit increase of up to 7% of their base salary.
Employees rated Needs Improvement or Unsatisfactory are not eligible for merit increases in that cycle.
All compensation decisions are subject to budget approval by the Finance department.

Section 4: Safety and Compliance Training
------------------------------------------
All employees must complete mandatory safety training within 30 days of joining the company.
Annual refresher safety training is required for all staff and must be completed by 31st March each year.
Employees in laboratory, manufacturing, or fieldwork roles must additionally complete role-specific safety certifications.
Non-compliance with mandatory training requirements may result in disciplinary action.
Training completion is tracked centrally by the Learning and Development (L&D) department.

Section 5: Code of Conduct
---------------------------
All employees are expected to behave professionally and with integrity at all times.
Discrimination, harassment, or bullying of any kind will not be tolerated and will result in immediate disciplinary proceedings.
Conflicts of interest must be declared to the Ethics and Compliance team promptly.
Employees must not accept gifts valued above £50 from clients, vendors, or partners without prior written approval from their department head.
Confidential company information must not be shared externally without explicit written authorization.

Section 6: Expense Reimbursement
----------------------------------
Business-related expenses must be submitted through the expense management system within 30 days of incurring the cost.
All expenses above £100 require a receipt and prior approval from the employee's line manager.
Travel expenses for domestic trips must not exceed the company's standard daily rate of £150 for accommodation and £40 for meals.
International travel requires advance approval from the department head and must be booked through the corporate travel portal.
Personal expenses will not be reimbursed under any circumstances.

Section 7: Grievance Procedure
--------------------------------
Employees who have a workplace grievance should first attempt informal resolution by speaking with their line manager.
If informal resolution fails, a formal grievance may be raised in writing to the HR department.
Formal grievances will be acknowledged within 5 working days and investigated within 20 working days.
The outcome of a formal grievance investigation will be communicated to the employee in writing.
Employees have the right to appeal any formal grievance decision within 10 working days of receiving the outcome.
